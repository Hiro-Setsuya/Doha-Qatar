DISCOVER
DOHA

Doha is the capital and most populous city of Qatar, with an estimated population of over 1.2 million (2024). It lies on the coast of the Arabian Gulf in the east of the country, north of Al Wakrah and south of Al Khor.

As Qatar’s economic, political, and cultural center, Doha is home to nearly 80% of the nation’s population and continues to expand rapidly through modern developments such as Lusail City and Msheireb Downtown.

The city was founded in the 1820s as an offshoot of Al Bidda and officially became Qatar’s capital in 1971, following the country’s independence from British protection.

MODERN ARCHITECTURE

Explore Doha's stunning blend of traditional and contemporary design

Burj Doha (Doha Tower)

Cylindrical skyscraper designed by Jean Nouvel with an intricate Islamic lattice façade that filters sunlight. Located in the West Bay business district.

The Pearl-Qatar

A man-made island featuring luxury residences, marinas, and Mediterranean-style architecture. Notable for its Venetian-inspired Qanat Quartier.

Lusail City

A planned smart city north of Doha, known for sustainable and high-tech architecture. Includes Lusail Stadium, host of the 2022 FIFA World Cup Final.

National Museum of Qatar

Designed by Jean Nouvel and inspired by desert rose crystals, its interlocking sand-colored discs create a breathtaking architectural form.

Museum of Islamic Art (MIA)

Designed by I.M. Pei, this masterpiece blends modern geometry with Islamic architectural traditions, overlooking Doha’s Corniche.

Katara Towers (Lusail Marina District)

Iconic crescent-shaped twin towers representing Qatar’s national emblem. Houses the luxurious Fairmont and Raffles Hotels.

Qatar National Library

Designed by Rem Koolhaas, this futuristic library features open spaces with bookshelves built directly into its structure.

Al Bidda Tower

A twisting glass skyscraper in West Bay, notable for its elegant curvature and striking modern design.

POPULAR LANDMARKS

Souq Waqif

A vibrant traditional market offering spices, textiles, and Qatari cuisine — restored to preserve 19th-century architectural style.

Katara Cultural Village

A hub for arts, music, and theatre featuring traditional architecture, an amphitheater, mosques, and galleries.

The Corniche

A scenic 7 km waterfront promenade offering panoramic views of Doha’s skyline, perfect for evening walks.

The Torch Doha

A 300m-tall hotel shaped like a torch — built for the 2006 Asian Games. Features a revolving restaurant and observation deck.

State Grand Mosque

Qatar’s largest mosque, blending traditional Islamic design with modern elements and elegant stone architecture.

Villaggio Mall

Venice-inspired shopping destination featuring indoor canals, gondola rides, and luxury international brands.

Education City

Home to world-class universities and modern architecture, including the Qatar Foundation HQ and Education City Mosque.

Aspire Park

One of Doha’s largest green spaces, offering lakes, playgrounds, and stunning views of the Torch Tower.

Lusail Stadium

An 80,000-seat venue for the 2022 FIFA World Cup Final, designed with golden motifs inspired by Arabic lanterns.
